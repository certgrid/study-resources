# Lab 07 - Explore the Service Trust Portal

The Service Trust Portal is a public website, so this is the easiest lab in the pack: no tenant, no cost, no risk. It directly covers an outline bullet.

## Time Needed

10-15 minutes.

## What You Will Learn

- What the Service Trust Portal offers
- Where to find Microsoft's certifications and audit reports
- How this differs from Compliance Manager
- Where Microsoft's privacy commitments are documented

## SC-900 Concepts Covered

| Concept                         | Where you see it in the lab          |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Service Trust Portal            | The site itself                      |
| Certifications and attestations | ISO, SOC, FedRAMP report listings    |
| Compliance evidence             | Audit reports and whitepapers        |
| Privacy principles              | Microsoft Trust Center privacy pages |

## Steps

### 1. Open the portal

1. Go to servicetrust.microsoft.com. No sign-in is needed for browsing.
2. Skim the sections on the home page.

### 2. Find certifications

1. Locate the certifications/regulations area (e.g. ISO/IEC, SOC).
2. Open the ISO/IEC section and find an ISO 27001 related report entry for Microsoft cloud services.
3. Note: DOWNLOADING some documents requires signing in with a Microsoft cloud account and accepting a non-disclosure notice. Browsing titles is enough for this lab.

### 3. Notice "your library"

1. Find the saved-documents/library feature description: signed-in users can save reports for later review. This "resources for your review" idea appears in exam wording.

### 4. Contrast with Compliance Manager

Answer this out loud:

| Question                                    | Answer            |
| ------------------------------------------- | ----------------- |
| Who is being proven compliant on this site? | Microsoft         |
| Who is measured by Compliance Manager?      | Your organization |

### 5. Visit the Trust Center privacy pages

1. From the portal, follow the link to the Microsoft Trust Center.
2. Find the privacy section and match what you read to the six principles: control, transparency, security, strong legal protections, no content-based targeting, benefits to you.

## Clean Up

Nothing to clean up; it is a public website.

## Success Criteria

You are done when you can explain:

- Three kinds of documents the Service Trust Portal offers
- Why an auditor would use the Service Trust Portal instead of Compliance Manager
- Where Microsoft's privacy principles are published
- What requires sign-in on the portal
